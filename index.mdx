<div className="mt-16 mb-16 max-w-5xl mx-auto px-6">
  <h2 className="text-2xl sm:text-3xl font-bold text-gray-900 dark:text-white mb-3">Quick Start</h2>
  <p className="text-base text-gray-600 dark:text-gray-400 mb-8">Get started with oUF in just a few steps</p>

  <Steps>
    <Step title="Download oUF">
      Download oUF from [WoWInterface](https://www.wowinterface.com/downloads/info9994-oUF.html) or [CurseForge](https://www.curseforge.com/wow/addons/ouf) and place it in your `Interface/AddOns/` directory.
    </Step>
    <Step title="Create Your Layout">
      Create a new addon that depends on oUF. In your TOC file, add:
      ```lua
      ## Dependencies: oUF
      ```
    </Step>
    <Step title="Register a Style">
      Create your first style function:
      ```lua
      local function MyStyle(frame, unit)
        -- Create health bar
        local Health = CreateFrame('StatusBar', nil, frame)
        Health:SetHeight(20)
        Health:SetPoint('TOP')
        Health:SetPoint('LEFT')
        Health:SetPoint('RIGHT')
        Health.colorHealth = true
        
        frame.Health = Health
      end

      oUF:RegisterStyle('MyLayout', MyStyle)
      oUF:SetActiveStyle('MyLayout')
      ```
    </Step>
    <Step title="Spawn Your Frames">
      Create unit frames for player, target, etc:
      ```lua
      oUF:Spawn('player', 'oUF_Player')
      oUF:Spawn('target', 'oUF_Target')
      ```
      
      Your frames will now appear in-game!
    </Step>
  </Steps>
</div>
